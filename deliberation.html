Internal Deliberation Process: Sharpening How We Communicate, Meet, and Gather

Why Are We Doing This?

At Change.org, we believe in practicing what we preach. Before we can help others run effective deliberation processes, we need to master them ourselves.

🔄 Dog-Fooding Our Own Process

We're testing deliberation tools and methodologies internally first, using existing third-party platforms to understand what works, what doesn't, and how we can improve. This hands-on experience will make us better at supporting the communities we serve.

🎯 Real Stakes, Real Impact

We chose a topic that genuinely matters to all of us—how we work together as a company. This isn't just a test run; your contributions will directly inform decisions that affect our daily work lives.

📋 Leadership Commitment

The C-team will review all outputs from this process and use them as valuable input for company-wide decisions. Your voice will be heard at the highest levels of the organization.

How This Works

We're launching a two-week collaborative process to improve how we work together as one company. Your voice matters, and we want to hear from everyone.

1

Week 1: Ideation

Everyone participates for 10-20 minutes to contribute ideas and vote on others' suggestions. Share your experiences and creative solutions.

2

Week 2: Deliberation

Selected participants will join a deeper, live conversation to refine the most promising proposals into actionable solutions.

The Challenge We're Solving

Problem Framing Question:

How can we work together more efficiently as one company by sharpening the way we communicate, meet, and gather in person?

Fictional Examples from Our Team

Team/Department Lead: "I want to limit the number of weeks where squad members are out (designer one week, PM the next, engineer after), making it hard to maintain momentum and alignment."
Senior Leadership: "I want comms channels that surface critical blockers fast yet keep deep-work hours free, so that I can make strategic decisions without drowning in notifications."
New Employee: "I want crystal-clear rules on where information lives and how fast to respond, so that I can onboard confidently without pinging people for 'where do I find X?'"
Cross-functional Project Lead: "I want core working hours reserved on everyone's calendar, so that scheduling live calls doesn't take a week of back-and-forth and late-night compromises."

What the Experts Say About This

Leading workplace efficiency researchers and organizational psychologists have identified key patterns in high-performing companies:

📚 Documentation Best Practices

  • MIT's Workplace Studies: Teams with centralized documentation systems are 40% more efficient at onboarding and 25% faster at project handoffs.
  • Stanford Research: "Single source of truth" systems reduce information-seeking time by an average of 2.3 hours per employee per week.

🤝 Meeting Science

  • Harvard Business Review: Companies with strict agenda requirements see 67% more actionable outcomes from meetings.
  • Microsoft's Work Trend Index: "No-meeting" blocks of 4+ hours correlate with 23% higher reported job satisfaction and productivity.

💬 Communication Patterns

  • UC Berkeley Study: Clear async communication protocols reduce "urgent" interruptions by 45% while maintaining response quality.
  • Slack's Future of Work Institute: Teams using structured communication channels (threads, scheduled sends) report 30% less communication fatigue.
  • Georgetown's Cal Newport: "Communication protocols aren't restrictions—they're the foundation that enables deep work to flourish."

⚡ Focus & Velocity Research

  • Deep Work Institute: Employees need an average of 23 minutes to refocus after an interruption—making protected time blocks essential.
  • Basecamp's Research: Companies with consolidated time-off periods (vs. scattered days) see 35% better post-break productivity and team cohesion.

What's In Scope vs. Out of Scope

✅ In Scope

  • Company-wide rules for Slack vs. Email usage
  • Documentation standards and source of truth
  • No-Meeting days and meeting guidelines
  • Summer shutdown vs. scattered Recharge Days
  • Agenda requirements for meetings
  • In-person gathering guidelines
  • Company rituals and practices

❌ Out of Scope

  • Org-chart changes or team mergers
  • Leadership structure adjustments
  • Tooling replacements (Slack, Gmail, etc.)
  • Team ownership or autonomy shifts
  • Compensation or performance policies
